AN ONE MINUTE STORY

A NEW
WALK
BEGAN

Jade Hatanaka has received many labels: at 19, whe she was at the Air Force Academy, she was known as "the girl who flew". At age 21, when she was diagnosed with herpetic meningoencephalitis, she was "the girl who survived". At 22, after she was discharged and discovered that she could no longer fly because of a stain that remained on her brain, Jade rediscovered herself as "the girl who coded" when she started a Computer Science course at UFRGS. These labels say a little, indeed, about this girl's story - and she is proud of each one of them - but they leave out what she considers the most important: Jade, above all, is the girl who, even when there was no ground, learned to walk again.
